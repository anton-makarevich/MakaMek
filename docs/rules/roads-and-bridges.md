## Roads and Paved Areas

In BattleTech, **pavement** and **roads** represent smooth, hard surfaces like asphalt, cement, or cobblestone that facilitate faster travel but introduce the hazard of losing control.

### **Definitions and Placement**
*   **Pavement:** Paved hexes include large areas such as landing fields, sidewalks, or entire urban blocks.
*   **Roads:** Roads are narrow strips of pavement that pass through other types of terrain (such as woods or rough).
*   **Bridges:** When a road passes over water or terrain lower than the road level, it is considered a bridge.
*   **Placement:** Roads can be placed through almost any terrain. For Line of Sight (LOS) and height purposes, pavement and roads are generally considered to be at the printed level of the hex.

### **Movement Rules for BattleMechs**
*   **Movement Cost:** Entering a paved or road hex costs **+0 MP** (total 1 MP).
*   **"On" the Road Status:** To be considered "on" the road and gain its benefits, a unit must move **from one road or paved hex to another** road or paved hex.
*   **Ignoring Underlying Terrain:** While "on" the road, a 'Mech **ignores the movement penalties and restrictions** of the underlying terrain. For example, a 'Mech can move through a heavy woods hex at the cost of only 1 MP if it stays on the road.
*   **Prohibited Terrain:** Units may move through terrain that is normally prohibited (like deep water via a bridge) as long as they stay on the road.
*   **Level Changes:** Units must still pay the standard MP cost for changing levels while entering a hex, even if following a road.
*   **Backward Movement:** Units moving backward cannot change levels, which includes depth changes on roads or bridges.

### **Important Gameplay & Combat Perspective**
*   **Skidding Risk:** If a 'Mech using **Running movement** makes a facing change in a paved/road hex and then attempts to enter a new hex, it must make a **Piloting Skill Roll (PSR)** to avoid skidding.
    *   **Failure Result:** The 'Mech falls and skids for a distance equal to half the hexes moved that turn (rounded up).
    *   **Damage:** Skidding 'Mechs take half their normal falling damage per hex traveled during the skid.
    *   **Combat Penalty:** A skidding unit is easier to hit (**+2 to-hit modifier**) and suffers a **+1 to-hit penalty** on its own attacks.
*   **Bridge Capacity:** Bridges have a **Construction Factor (CF)**. If the total tonnage of units on a bridge hex exceeds its current CF, the bridge **collapses**, and the units take standard falling damage to the terrain below.
*   **Vehicle Bonus:** While 'Mechs do not get a speed boost on pavement, **ground vehicles** (wheeled/tracked) receive a **+1 MP bonus** if they begin their turn on pavement and stay on it for their entire movement.
*   **Airborne Units:** VTOLs and WiGE vehicles can use roads to traverse woods hexes at an elevation **below the treetops** without crashing, provided they stay on the road for the entire movement through the woods.
*   **Aerospace Landing:** Pavement and roads are the safest places for aerospace units to land, providing a **0 terrain modifier** to the landing Control Roll.

## Bridge Levels

A unit in a hex containing both a bridge and water can be located in one of two vertical positions: **on top of the bridge** or **underneath the bridge**.

### **1. On Top of the Bridge**
When a unit is "on" the bridge, it is traveling at the **road level**.
*   **Terrain Interaction:** The unit **ignores the normal terrain restrictions** and movement penalties of the underlying terrain (such as water MP costs or depth-based Piloting Skill Rolls).
*   **Weight Limits:** Each hex of the bridge has a Construction Factor (CF) representing the weight it can bear. If the total tonnage of units on the bridge in a single hex exceeds the current CF, that hex **collapses**, and the units fall to the underlying terrain (water), taking standard falling damage.

### **2. Underneath the Bridge**
A unit may enter a bridge hex and be considered "underneath" the bridge, provided the level of the underlying hex plus the height of the unit is **equal to or less than the level of the bridge**. In this position, the unit is interacting with the water terrain. The unit's specific location depends on its type:
*   **'Mechs:** The unit is located at the **bottom (bed) of the hex**. It must follow standard rules for underwater movement and depth-based penalties.
*   **Surface Naval Vessels:** The unit is located on the **surface of the water**.
*   **Submarines or Units using UMU MP:** The unit is located at its **current depth** within the water.

### **Determining Position**
*   **Road Movement:** A unit is considered "on" the bridge if it moves from one road or paved hex to another road or paved hex.
*   **Off-Road Movement:** A unit not traveling "on" the road is considered to be moving on the **underlying terrain** (underneath the bridge).
*   **Insufficient Clearance:** If a unit cannot fit underneath the bridge due to its height and is not moving on top of it, the bridge is treated as a **building hex** for movement purposes.
